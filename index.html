Welcome to USC 2023: University Scholars Conference
ABOUT THE EVENT
USC is a premier academic event organized by the Association of Mechanical Engineering Students (AMES), aimed at fostering a culture of research and innovation among undergraduate students in Nepal. Since its inception in 2021, USC has been at the forefront of providing a scholarly platform for undergraduate students to showcase their cutting-edge developments and state-of-the-art research work in the field of science and engineering. With a strong focus on academic excellence, USC serves as a catalyst for the growth and recognition of the next generation of engineering scholars. This year, we are thrilled to announce that USC 2023 will be held in a physical mode, allowing participants to engage in vibrant discussions, networking opportunities, and collaborative activities.

CONFERENCE CHAIR
Prof. Dr. Hari Prasad Neopane
Conference Chair for USC 2023
Prof. Dr. Hari Prasad Neopane is a professor at the Department of Mechanical Engineering, Kathmandu University, Nepal. He has been working at the Department of Mechanical Engineering, Kathmandu University, since 2000 and serving the university with different administrative responsibilities, which include International Master Program Coordinator, Head of Department, Associate Dean of the School of Engineering, Member Secretary of the Turbine Testing Lab, etc.

His fields of interest are hydropower turbine design and sediment erosion. He received his PhD in fluid engineering from the Department of Energy and Process Engineering, NTNU, Norway, in 2010. He has published more than 100 research articles and three book chapters in national and international journals and conference proceedings. He has received the Education Award (Shikshya Puraskar), the Young Scientist Award, and many more from the government of Nepal for the dedication of his service and contribution in the education sector of Nepal.

Prof. Neopane has been coordinating several research projects, which include an Erasmus + project on capacity building, the Norwegian Research Council, the Royal Norwegian Embassy, NORAD, the Korea International Cooperation Agency (KOICA), the Nepal Electricity Authority, UGC Nepal, and many more. He has also handled a number of national and international conferences and training courses related to his field of interest. He has also been supervising a large number of master’s and PhD students in Nepal and abroad and guiding them in their research endeavors. He has visited more than 25 countries for research and development as well as international collaboration and cooperation.

As a dedicated educator, Prof. Neopane has played a vital role in shaping the curriculum and academic programs at Kathmandu University. His teaching methodologies emphasize practical application and critical thinking, equipping students with the skills necessary for real-world engineering challenges. He is known for his engaging lectures that encourage students to explore innovative solutions and nurture their passion for mechanical engineering. He has served on various committees and advisory boards, offering his expertise to promote academic excellence and research advancement in the field of mechanical engineering.
